E-Commerce Customer Behavior & Sales Analysis (Power BI)
Project Overview

This project focuses on analyzing customer behavior and sales performance for an e-commerce platform using Power BI. The objective of the analysis is to transform raw event-level data into meaningful business insights that help stakeholders understand user engagement, pricing trends, promotional impact, and product performance.

The dashboard enables data-driven decision-making by providing clear visualizations of key performance indicators (KPIs) related to revenue, traffic, product activity, and brand performance.

Business Objective

An e-commerce client wanted to understand how customer behavior, pricing strategies, and promotional activities influence sales performance. The goal was to build an analytical dashboard that helps business teams monitor KPIs, evaluate marketing effectiveness, and identify opportunities for growth.

Key Business Questions Addressed

How does pricing vary across brands, categories, time, and sales channels?

How does customer traffic vary by day, time, and channel (App vs Browser)?

What are the key business KPIs such as revenue, product activity, and category performance?

What patterns exist in brand preference and brand activity?

How do customers search for products by brand or category?

What impact do special promotions have on user engagement and sales?

How do pricing fluctuations influence purchase behavior?

Dataset Description

The project uses two datasets:

1. Sales_Data_Ecommerce

Contains event-level customer behavior data including:

User activity (view, cart, purchase)

Product details (brand, category, price)

Event information (date, time, channel)

Customer segmentation and location data

2. Promotions Dataset

Contains promotional campaign data including:

Promotion type

Discount percentage

Product promoted

Promotion date

Key Analysis Performed

Built KPI dashboards to monitor revenue, traffic, and product activity

Analyzed pricing trends across brands, categories, and time

Evaluated traffic patterns by day, time, and channel

Investigated the relationship between promotions and sales activity

Explored brand and category search behavior

Assessed the impact of pricing changes on purchase decisions
